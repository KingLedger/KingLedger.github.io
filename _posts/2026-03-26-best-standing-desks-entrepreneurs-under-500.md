## What Makes a Good Standing Desk?

Before the picks, here's what separates a good standing desk from a wobbly, creaky waste of money:

### Motor Quality
- **Single motor:** Cheaper, slower, and louder. Fine for occasional adjustments.
- **Dual motor:** Faster, quieter, smoother, and more stable. Worth the extra cost.

### Height Range
- Minimum standing height should accommodate users up to 6'4" (at least 48 inches)
- Minimum sitting height should go low enough for shorter users (around 25-27 inches)

### Desktop Size
- **48" x 24":** Minimum for a monitor, keyboard, and a few accessories
- **55" x 28"** or larger: Ideal for dual monitors and a full workspace
- **60" x 30":** The power user setup

### Weight Capacity
- 150 lbs minimum for basic setups
- 250+ lbs if you're running dual monitors, a desktop PC, and accessories

### Stability
- The desk should not wobble at standing height. Period. This is where cheap desks fail.

## Standing Desk Setup Tips for Maximum Productivity

Getting the desk is step one. Setting it up correctly is step two.

### Monitor Height
Your screen should be at eye level when standing. If it's not, get a monitor arm or a laptop stand. Looking down at a screen for hours defeats the purpose of standing.

### Anti-Fatigue Mat
Standing on a hard floor for hours is almost as bad as sitting. Invest $30-$50 in an anti-fatigue mat. Your knees and lower back will thank you.

### The 30/30 Rule
Stand for 30 minutes, sit for 30 minutes. That's the rhythm. Don't try to stand for 8 hours straight on day one — your body needs time to adapt.

### Cable Management
A standing desk with cables everywhere looks terrible and creates hazards when the desk adjusts. Use a cable management tray or Velcro ties to keep everything clean.

### Keyboard and Mouse Position
Your elbows should be at 90 degrees when typing, whether sitting or standing. If your desk height is right but your keyboard position isn't, a keyboard tray fixes the problem.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are standing desks actually worth it?

Yes — if you use them correctly. The key is alternating between sitting and standing throughout the day. Studies show that sit-stand desk users report less back pain, more energy, and better focus. The ROI on a $300 desk that keeps you healthier and more productive for years is enormous.

### How long should I stand at a standing desk?

Start with 15-30 minute intervals and build up. Most ergonomics experts recommend a 1:1 or 2:1 sit-to-stand ratio. Listen to your body — if your feet or legs are fatiguing, sit down. The goal is movement variation, not endurance standing.

### Can I use a standing desk with a treadmill?

Yes, and it's an excellent combination. A walking pad under a standing desk lets you walk 1-2 mph while working. You won't break a sweat but you'll burn significantly more calories and keep blood flowing. Just make sure your desk's height range accommodates the treadmill height.

### Do standing desks wobble?

Cheap ones can. The main factors are frame quality, crossbar support, and desktop size. The desks on this list were chosen partly for stability. Dual motor desks tend to be more stable than single motor. If you notice wobble, check that all bolts are fully tightened and the desk is on a level surface.

### What's the ideal desk size for a home office?

For a single monitor setup: 48" x 24" minimum. For dual monitors: 55" x 28" or larger. For a full command center (dual monitors + laptop + peripherals): 60" x 30". Always measure your space before buying — leave at least 24" of clearance behind the desk for your chair.
