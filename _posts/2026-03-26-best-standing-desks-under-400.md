These are the **best standing desks under $400** — tested for stability, motor quality, desktop size, and overall value.

## FAQ

### How long should I stand vs sit during the day?

The general recommendation is a **2:1 sit-to-stand ratio** — for every 60 minutes of sitting, stand for 30 minutes. As your body adapts, you can increase standing time. Most people settle into a rhythm of alternating every 30-60 minutes. Listen to your body — if your feet hurt, sit down.

### Do standing desks actually help with back pain?

Research shows that sit-stand desks can reduce lower back pain by 32-54% when used consistently. The key is *alternating* — standing all day is just as bad as sitting all day. The ability to change positions throughout the day is what provides relief.

### Are budget standing desks stable enough for dual monitors?

Most desks on this list (especially the FlexiSpot E7 Pro and Uplift V2) are stable enough for dual-monitor setups. Look for desks with 150+ lb weight capacity and three-stage legs for the best stability. The cheapest desks may wobble slightly with heavy setups at max height.

### How loud are electric standing desks?

Modern dual-motor desks operate at 40-50dB — roughly the volume of a quiet conversation. You can adjust height during video calls without disrupting audio. Single-motor desks are slightly louder and slower but still manageable.

### Is it hard to assemble a standing desk?

Most desks take 20-45 minutes with basic tools (usually just a Phillips screwdriver and the included Allen key). The frame assembly is straightforward — attach the legs, mount the motor, attach the desktop, and plug it in. Instructions vary in quality, but YouTube assembly videos exist for most popular models.
