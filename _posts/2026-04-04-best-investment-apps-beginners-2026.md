- **Found Money**: Cashback from 350+ partner brands

**If you're completely new to investing**: Start with **Acorns** or **Betterment**. They handle everything automatically while you learn.

**If you want to buy individual stocks**: Go with **Robinhood** for simplicity or **M1 Finance** for more sophisticated portfolio management.

**If you want to learn while you invest**: **Stash** combines education with thematic investing that's easy to understand.

**If you want maximum automation**: **Wealthfront** offers the most advanced hands-off wealth management.

**If you want an all-in-one financial solution**: **SoFi** handles investing, banking, and financial planning in one platform.

## FAQ

**Q: How much money do I need to start investing?**  
A: Most of these apps let you start with $1-5. The key is starting, not the amount.

**Q: Is it safe to keep my money in these apps?**  
A: Yes. All are SIPC insured up to $500,000, and your investments are held by major custodians like Apex Clearing or Pershing.

**Q: Should I use multiple apps?**  
A: Start with one to avoid confusion. You can always diversify across platforms later as your wealth grows.

**Q: What if the stock market crashes right after I start?**  
A: Market downturns are buying opportunities, not disasters. Dollar-cost averaging (investing the same amount regularly) automatically buys more shares when prices are low.

**Q: How much should I invest each month?**  
A: Start with whatever doesn't stress you out—even $25-50 per month. The habit matters more than the amount initially.
