## FAQ

**Do whiteboards really help with goal achievement?**

Research consistently shows that writing goals down and making them visible increases achievement rates. A Harvard Business Study (often cited, debated in methodology, but directionally correct) found that the 3% of graduates who wrote down their goals earned ten times as much as the other 97% combined. Dr. Gail Matthews' more rigorous study at Dominican University confirmed that written, visible goals are achieved significantly more often than unwritten ones.

**Glass board or standard whiteboard?**

Standard whiteboards are cheaper and lighter. Glass boards don't ghost, don't stain, look better, and last forever. If budget is tight, start with a standard board. If you're investing in your workspace long-term, glass is worth the upgrade.

**What size whiteboard should I get?**

For a dedicated goal board: at least 36×24 inches. For a command center: 48×36 or larger. The most common regret is buying too small. You'll always fill the space you have.

**How do I prevent ghosting on non-glass whiteboards?**

Clean weekly with a whiteboard cleaning spray (or a 50/50 mix of water and isopropyl alcohol). Erase within 48 hours of writing — ink left for weeks is harder to remove. Use high-quality markers (EXPO brand specifically).

**Can I use these boards for financial tracking?**

Absolutely. Many people use wall whiteboards to track: net worth monthly, debt payoff progress, savings rate, investment portfolio value, income vs. expenses, and side hustle revenue. Making financial numbers visible creates powerful accountability.

**What about digital alternatives like Notion or Trello?**

Digital tools are great for detailed project management. But they're invisible unless you actively open them. Physical boards are ambient — they're always in your peripheral vision, constantly reinforcing your commitments. The best system uses both: physical boards for high-level goals and daily priorities, digital tools for detailed task management.
