## FAQ

### How much protein do I actually need per day?

The research consistently supports **0.7–1g of protein per pound of bodyweight** for active lifters. If you weigh 180 lbs, aim for 130–180g per day. Protein powder can help you fill the gap between what you eat and what you need.

### Is whey isolate really better than whey concentrate?

Isolate is more refined — more protein per gram, less fat, less lactose. But concentrate still works. If you digest dairy fine and aren't counting every calorie, concentrate (like Naked Whey) is perfectly effective and often cheaper.

### When is the best time to take protein powder?

Post-workout is the classic answer, and it's valid — your muscles are primed for nutrient uptake. But total daily protein intake matters more than timing. Use it whenever it helps you hit your target: morning shake, post-gym, before bed (casein), or between meals.

### Can I build muscle with plant-based protein?

Yes. Plant-based proteins have come a long way. The key is choosing a blend (like Vega Sport) that combines multiple plant sources for a complete amino acid profile. You may need a slightly higher serving to match whey gram-for-gram, but it absolutely works.

### Do protein powders have side effects?

For most people, no. The most common issue is digestive discomfort from whey concentrate if you're lactose-sensitive — in which case, switch to isolate or plant-based. Protein powders are food supplements, not drugs. If you're healthy and within recommended doses, you're fine.
